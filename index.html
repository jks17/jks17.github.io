I am a PhD candidate in Computing at Imperial College London, supervised by Prof. Michael Bronstein and Dr. Kirill Veselkov. I am part of the AI4Health CDT where my research lies at the intersection of machine learning and precision medicine. I am particularly interested in applying and developing geometric deep learning methods and its various applications such as the computational personalisation and generation of health promoting hyperfoods.

Prior to my PhD, I did my Masters degree in Applied Mathematics, also at Imperial College London, and then spent two years working as a Machine Learning Researcher at a Health Tech startup called Limbic trying to model human psychology with applications to mental health

Music and the brain

Read Thesis

Used tools from Information Theory (PMIME) to create a time-varying causal network between EEG time series. I then used network science approaches, particularly the Shannon entropy of the distribution of community sizes, to analyze differences in brain function for musicians when they are either improvising or playing a set piece.

Presented at NetSci 2019.

Emotion from smartwatch data

Read Paper

Extended work on emotion recognition from medical grade ECG devices to PPG collected from a smartwatch. Made statistical comparisons of the ECG and PPG time-series and used Transfer Learning to try and improve model performance.

Poster presented at ACII 2019.

Bayesian deep learning

Read Paper

Bayesian deep learning for emotion recognition. Used an LSTM-FCN to improve state of the art accuracy for predicting emotional valence from unimodal heartbeat data. Incorporated Bayesian considerations into the model using Monte-Carlo dropout for uncertainty quantification and to tune the accuracy and coverage.

Regret Learning - Colonol Blotto

Implemented a regret learning algorithm to approximately solve the Colonol Blotto Game. This a useful model for optimising the allocation of resources.

Source code available on GitHub.

Bsc Thesis

An investigation of Bound States in the Absence of a Confining Potential. Used computational methods such as Finite Differences to calculate binding potentials in some 2D structures.
